A private housing community with only 56 individual residence buildings, Stuyvesant Town is an enclave within New York City. Stuyvesant Town is bordered by Gramercy Park and East Village.

Well known for its distinctive architecture that was collectively built as part of a housing crisis, the private Stuyvesant Town community even features its own security and newspaper from the outside world. When inside, you will experience the luxury of eating riverside at one of the many restaurants here, as well as enjoying a beer and a view when the sun sets. With many international schools gracing the Stuyvesant Town neighborhood, you are sure to get a premier education for your children at one of these prestigious schools. Regardless of why you’d like to live in Stuyvesant Town, the neighborhood and amenities here speak for themselves.

If you’re looking to buy or rent here, you’ll be lucky to find a property available to suit your tastes, as there is a considerably lower turnaround rate in this neighborhood. But, if you do, you will definitely be satisfied with the community feel of Stuyvesant Town, and the high demand makes it a great investment for the future!

River Park

For fine dining in Stuyvesant Town, visit any of these selections and they will not disappoint.

First, River Park is a restaurant located on the East River, which uses fresh produce and meats to create their exquisite dishes at reasonable prices. Some dishes of choice here include Squid Ink Chitarra and pork chops, which you can savor while sitting outside on the river, or in the modern interior. Come check out River Park in Stuyvesant Town today!

Or, perhaps The Water Club would better suit you, as it has a spectacular view of Queens at its waterfront location. The Water Club is especially known for its seafood, which includes Lobster Bisque and tuna as especially delicious. Although somewhat pricey, the romantic ambience will make up for the price.

If you’re in the mood for Belgian food, Petite Abeille will live up to your expectations. The fries and mussels here are especially fantastic, and overall this is a great place to eat brunch at, even if it is a bit overpriced. Plus, come on Mondays to receive a 50% discount on Belgian beers!


In terms of takeout in Stuyvesant Town, Bangkok 2 Thai combines American and Chinese food for the dish of your choice. The Momma’s Fried Chicken and Kee Mao are particularly exceptional, all for an affordable price. Even though the space is small, you can sit-down to eat if you’d like, or enjoy their fast delivery.

Bangkok 2 Thai

Or, try Murray’s Falafel for Middle Eastern food that you won’t soon forget. With options of Kosher food, everyone can enjoy the Tahini and Falafel here. At a reasonable price, you can even afford to pay for delivery, which will certainly come on time. Overall, this small restaurant is not one to forget.

For some amazing fresh sushi, check out Hane Sushi. With drink specials, fast delivery, and fresh food, you definitely have to try this restaurant. If you choose to eat here, enjoy the outdoor area with a relaxing ambience.


O'Hanlon's Bar

For a lovely bar experience, visit O’Hanlons. The sports bar atmosphere sets the tone here, complete with a pool table, darts, and fun jukebox where you can request songs. The bar itself is very long, so its able to accommodate many patrons here. Come during happy hour, from 11-8, and enjoy discounted prices.

Or, try The Crow’s Nest, located on the upper deck of The water Club. The views of the East River are tremendous here, and the bar is never that crowded, so you can cherish the views in peace. Although the bar’s a little pricey, the staff is attentive, and the drinks are also superb.

United Nations International School

If your child is looking to get an education in Stuyvesant Town, the international schools here are your best options.

One of these choices is United Nations International School, which enrolls students from elementary school to high school. This rigorous school prepares students for the SATs and beyond with their curriculum, and seeks to help every student preserve their individual culture, as students here are from over 80 different countries. As an establishment of Stuyvesant Town since 1947, you can rest assured that the level of education here is impeccable.

Another international school is British International School New York, which is well known for its state of the art technology. The atmosphere here is academic yet inspired, helping students to achieve their goals without pushing them too hard. The diverse curriculum helps students learn an international perspective on education, and this school services all international students.


Waterside Health Club

For a gym in Stuyvesant Town, visit Waterside Swim & Health Club. Some of their best features here include an indoor pool, fitness areas for kids, personal training, a roof deck, and tons of space for adults to work out at. With multiple full weight sets, many people are able to work out at the same time without it ever getting too crowded. Plus, the dance floor is extra spacious, so you can enjoy a class here as well! Regardless of your favorite workout activity, Waterside Swim & Health Club will definitely provide the activity and space for you to exercise at.


Stuyvesant Cove Park

If you’re looking for a park in Stuyvesant Town, perhaps Stuyvesant Cove Park suits your tastes. Here you can view both Williamsburg and Ed Koch Queensboro Bridge, which is always a wonderful way to relax. Also, here you can play baseball at their baseball field, or cook with friends in the barbecue area. Therefore, Stuyvesant Cove Park was created with intention to entertain both children and adults, so help the community fulfill their purpose by giving it a try today!
