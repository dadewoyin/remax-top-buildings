A combination of a gorgeous location, fabulous dining spots, and hip Chelsea art galleries make Chelsea stand out from the New York City crowd. Located next to Midtown South, West Village, and Midtown West, Chelsea is definitely in the center of the action while still maintaining a unique voice.

As for the history of Chelsea, the neighborhood was a sanctuary for the LGBT community, which was essentially threatened for a long time in New York City. Therefore, Chelsea is now one of the most accepting and open cultures, perfect for any personality type or background.

Today, you can enjoy Chelsea by strolling through one of its parks, shopping at some of the hippest stores in New York City, or checking out an iconic Chelsea art gallery. Some of the highlights of this neighborhood are their performances, namely Sleep No More and Joyce Theatre. Sleep No More is a revolutionary and iconic production that offers an interactive performance of the play MacBeth. The entire play, from its backdrop to amazing actors, will provide you with a great night of excitement. Or, a more refined option is Joyce Theatre, which features various exhibitions of dance productions that are loved by all of New York City.

In terms of real estate, the prices of Chelsea are actually decreasing, making it a perfect time to buy at the bottom of the market. Still, the housing and rental prices are above New York City average, so you will have to pay for access to such an iconic and wonderful neighborhood.

Regardless of what attracts you about Chelsea, follow your gut and look into some of their real estate opportunities today!

Colicchio & Sons Main Dining Room

Whether you’re looking for Italian, American, or Spanish food, Chelsea has the perfect fine dining location for you.

For a tremendous American restaurant that is owned by Top Chef judge Tom Colicchio, visit Colicchio & Sons Main Dining Room. Their service alone is something to rave about, not to mention the amazing food. All dishes are served as appetizers, so you will have plenty of stomach space to try out new dishes for a fraction of the cost. All elements of this restaurant are genius, and Colicchio & Sons will not disappoint.

Another superb choice is Scarpetta, an Italian restaurant located right in Chelsea. Another TV star, the owner Scott Conant is one of the judges on Chopped, and is notoriously hard on subpar pasta dishes, which is probably because his restaurant serves some of the best pasta in New York City! Make sure that you try a variety of appetizers and entrees around your table to experience the full grandeur of the restaurant.

Or, if you’re interested in some great Spanish cuisine, check out Salinas. Residents love what they consider the best sangria in the area, as the mix contains a good ratio of liquor instead of being primarily sweet. Be sure to share a lot of tapas among your group, as there are many unique tastes to try.


If you’re looking for a quick bite to eat, Chelsea will not disappoint with its large collection of restaurants in the neighborhood.

Whether you’d like your coffee hot or iced (with coffee ice cubes), this refreshing drink will surely satisfy. You can also enjoy one of their fresh pastries while there if you’d like.

Westville

If you’re tired of indulgences and would like to cleanse your palate with something healthy, Westville is the ideal stop for you. With their many additions and substitutions, you can definitely create the perfect meal for you.

Rubin Museum of Art

With a reputation for a neighborhood of entertainment and enjoyment, Chelsea offers its fair share of attractions that you will definitely love.

One of the most famous locations in Chelsea is The Joyce Theatre, a premier dance hall that showcases the beauty of performance. People flock from all over New York City for their beautiful performances, and are constantly offering new shows for you to visit. The theatre itself is beautiful and intimate, and will certainly supply a tremendous atmosphere for you to appreciate.

Another museum in the area is Rubin Museum of Art, which focuses on the art and culture of the Himalayas. The venue features exhibitions and performances of many varieties, which are perfect to learn about the culture in a new light. Besides its marvelous offerings of art, this museum has a fantastic bar and cafe, which only add to the appeal.

One of the most groundbreaking shows is Sleep No More, and with their performance location in Chelsea, this is the ideal place to stop while in the area. Centered around the story of MacBeth, this interactive performance takes you around a mansion of acting and mystery for a play like you’ve never seen before.

Although the exhibits are constantly changing, you will have an opportunity to see many different styles of art-something for everyone to marvel at.

Chelsea also boasts some great hospitals within the area, so any health concerns that you may have will be easily taken care of within their facilities.

One of the best hospitals in the Chelsea neighborhood isn’t for humans. The Heart of Chelsea Animal Hospital is known for its high tech solutions to animal health issues, through use of acupuncture, radiology, herbology, pet laser therapy, and many more resources. Whether you choose a traditional option or a holistic one, Heart of Chelsea will take good care of your pet.

As the historically most LGBT friendly neighborhood in all of New York City, Chelsea’s health services mirror its open-minded attitude. Callen-Lorde Community Health Center specifically helps anyone who is lesbian, gay, bisexual, or transgender to receive the medical attention that they need, regardless of whether or not they are able to pay. As advocates for the LGBT community, Callen-Lorde is one of the staple Chelsea resources.

Chelsea Park

Chelsea Park is a great spot located right in Chelsea which is very popular and beautiful! With amenities including baseball fields, playgrounds, basketball courts, fitness equipment, and handball courts, there are countless activities to explore at Chelsea Park. Or, many people appreciate the simple pleasure of relaxing in the park and taking a break from the hustle and bustle of the New York City lifestyle.

If you’re interested in a park that is filled with history, then check out the historical Chelsea Waterside Park. After many transformations, the park now includes a dog run, basketball courts, recreational area, and playground, as well as a beautiful lawn and walking area for those who are interested in unwinding. Regardless of which activity you enjoy, be sure to stop by this serene location today.
