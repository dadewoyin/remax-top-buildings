Midtown East is a neighborhood located in the heart of New York City, and bordered by Lenox Hill, Murray Hill, and Midtown. The area is known as a jointly professional and residential environment, meaning that it is constantly abuzz with activity.

In terms of attractions, many people recognize the area by its iconic buildings, such as the Chrysler Building, Empire State Building, and Saint Patrick’s Cathedral. Midtown East is also known for its large international community due to the United Nations’ and other consulates’ presence. Because of the many attractions within the small area, the number of Midtown East restaurants match the congregate of people, so there is always something new to see or eat!

This particular part of Midtown is known as the corporate hub of New York City and thus it is very busy by day, with commuters going in and out of the train stations. The residential community comes to life at night, and therefore the nightlife scene is very active in Midtown East. With so many people present in the neighborhood, it is only natural for there to be many luxury boutiques and famous department stores right on 5th Avenue!

Some of the most desirable real estate is present in Midtown East and many international investors and residents have been scooping up the already hot real estate available. It’s safe to say Midtown East will have a prosperous market in the foreseeable future, so consider a property in Midtown East today.

Morgan Library

A complex of buildings in the heart of New York City, The Morgan Library & Museum began as the private library of financier Pierpont Morgan, one of the preeminent collectors and cultural benefactors in the United States. As early as 1890 Morgan had begun to assemble a collection of illuminated, literary, and historical manuscripts, early printed books, and old master drawings and prints. Don’t miss out on rare medieval manuscripts, ancient tablets, and various other historical artifacts.

The New York Transit Museum Gallery Annex & Store doubles as a trinket shop where you can find many subway themed items to a museum with small exhibits about train systems around the world. Housed inside Grand Central Station, there is no better place to learn more about the transit system!

Founded in 2002, 59E59 Theaters presents Off-Broadway productions by not-for-profit companies from across the United States and around the world. The theatre is a spectacular modern complex with three stages and a lively bar on the mezzanine level. There are three distinct venues inside, where you can enjoy multiple plays throughout the year!


If you want to broaden your cultural horizon, don’t hesitate to visit the Korean Cultural Service NY. This unique art gallery contains some of the most profound Korean art and educated cultural professionals to enlighten you about Korean culture. In addition to the various art works, the gallery has regular movie showings on classics from Korean cinema. The incredible library contains a collection of hundreds of Korean works and a flood of information on the interesting culture.
