Lenox Hill is a neighborhood located on the Upper East Side, next to Central Park South, and Midtown East. This area is known for its upscale atmosphere, including restaurants, shopping, and house prices.

One of the main attractions in Lenox Hill for visitors is the beautiful art stored in the Frick Collection. If you’re interested in Renaissance art, one of the most elegant and influential periods in art history, the Frick Collection is perfect for you. Or, if you’d prefer to do some shopping while in Lenox Hill, the opportunities to find upscale items are endless: from Kate Spade to Anthropologie, you are bound to find a quality store that you enjoy.

The residential makeup of Lenox Hill makes it the environment perfect for young families or postgraduates, as it is a step away from the commotion of Midtown, yet still close to the action. Families are attracted to the amazing school systems in the neighborhood, which is a huge draw. Residents can also feel safe in terms of their health, as the Lenox Hill Hospital has been standing for over a hundred years. The Lenox Hill Hospital is renowned for their dedication to patient care and quality service, and the Lenox Hill radiology is one of the best in all of New York City.

Because of the huge allure of the many Lenox Hill amenities, prices for the area are among the most expensive in New York City. Lenox Hill also recently ranked first in richest neighborhoods of New York City, so its no surprise that this neighborhood is filled with big money. If you’d like to buy or rent your way into this fabulous environment, be prepared to pay a hefty price.

For the more expensive side of Lenox Hill, there are many upscale restaurants that can meet your tastes.

If you are looking for more of a splurge for dinner, try The Simone. This upscale French spot not only boasts amazing food but also creates the perfect romantic atmosphere for you to enjoy an evening with a significant other. They are also known for establishing amazing wine pairings to perfect the night.

For another upscale dining experience, Daniel would make an excellent choice. With a two-star Michelin rating, this restaurant lives up to its expectations through its drinks and service. Their signature 7-course meal offers many different tastes, all a product of French cuisine.

If you’re still hungry for dessert, look to Lady M Boutique. Their signature is a mille crepe cake that has 20 layers of crepes in between light layers of pastry cream and caramelized around the outside. Although expensive, every bite will be worth the price, as this is one of the best cakes of New York City.

For a fun way to spend your day, Lenox Hill provides many attractions that service the neighborhood as points of interest.

If you’re interested in an all-around amazing display of the arts, visit French Institute Alliance Francaise while in Lenox Hill. This is one of the few places that creates a display of the Gallic culture and language, and offers shows, theater, and dance as entertainment. Or, perhaps you may enjoy The Grolier Club, a library that hosts many events in the area in the name of supporting books and printing as an art form. They also present many exhibitions on the history of the printing in the library, including personal collections of bibliographies that were created specifically for the library.

Regardless of which attraction you use, Lenox Hill will certainly provide some great entertainment for you to enjoy!

Families of Lenox Hill will be overjoyed by the level of education they experience at the public schools. From Eleanor Roosevelt High School to PS 183 and PS 167, there are many options to choose from to get a premier education for your child.

The best public high school of Lenox Hill is Eleanor Roosevelt High School, which contains about 500 students. With the difficult admissions, Lenox Hill students have an advantage of getting into this school because priority is giving to students of this neighborhood. They offer many challenging classes and fantastic college placement, making Eleanor Roosevelt one of the best public high schools of New York City.

Students in elementary school will be very happy at PS 183 Robert L. Stevenson Elementary School. With great middle school placement, this elementary school provides a strong core of education and an emphasis in the performance arts should the student be interested. Many students from this elementary school choose MS 167 Robert F. Wagner Middle School, which is one of the top middle schools.

Regardless of their level of education, your children will experience a high quality of education throughout their early years.


If you’re interested in remaining in the Lenox Hill neighborhood for college, look no further than the College University of New York Hunter College. The largest CUNY of New York City, Hunter offers many degrees at a discounted cost for in-state students, making this choice affordable and practical. The college currently enrolls around 20,000 students, and the admissions rates are only about 31%, making it selective. Hunter College primarily is known for its degrees offered in performing arts, but also has a variety of other degrees and master’s programs. This college has a special focus on maintaining the presence of women and minorities within the college, and extend many services and distinctions to preserve this balance.

So, if you’re looking to stay within the beauty of Lenox Hill, Hunter College may be perfect for you.
