We’ve all heard of Times Square and the Theater District because of the stories of the bright lights, busy streets, and tall buildings that people talk about when referring to their dream of living in NYC. Then you also have Hell’s Kitchen, which has a nightlife scene that helps NYC live up to its title of the city that never sleeps. What you probably didn’t know about Hell’s Kitchen, Times Square, and Theater District is that they are all neighborhoods in Midtown West.

Bordered by Chelsea, Midtown South, Turtle Bay, and Murray Hill, Midtown West is one of the most desirable and popular neighborhoods that is iconic to New York City.

Midtown West has many interesting attractions, including Rockefeller Center, home to Radio City Music Hall and Carnegie Hall, which are among the most prestigious performance spaces in existence. With Herald Square also located within the neighborhood, there are plenty of places to enjoy, both day and night. In addition to the fabulous venues located within Midtown West, the neighborhood also boasts great food and nightlife entertainment. Many Michelin-star restaurants take residence in Midtown West, as well as lively bars that are open for most of the night.

In terms of real estate, Midtown West is a central point of Manhattan, as almost every main NYC Subway line goes through there. This makes the properties very attractive due to the location and proximity to nightlife, which is reflected in the expensive prices of the neighborhood. With spacious room for growth in a lively and engaged neighborhood, Midtown West is the perfect spot to call home.

Museum of Modern Art

One of the most renowned museums of the Midtown West area is the Museum of Modern Art. Featuring many famous artists, including Monet, Warhol, Picasso, Van Gogh, and more, MOMA is one of the few great museums of New York City that you have to visit. If you enjoy the artwork by the most famous artists, they have dedicated a room to Monet’s Water Lilies, as well as various works by Picasso and Van Gogh. Many people choose to visit MOMA just to see the famous painting Starry Night, and the crowds are always huge around this exhibit.

Watching Phantom of the Opera at the Majestic Theater is a once in a lifetime experience. Phantom of the Opera is the longest running Broadway show to date, which is no surprise due to the amazing choreography and acting. With breathtaking scenes and a fluid setting, everyone must watch this show at least once.


If you’re interested in artwork, Midtown West will definitely live up to your expectations.

First, check out Christie’s, an auction house that functions like a gallery with furniture, artwork, and sculptures. There is a ton of art at this site that is constantly circulating, of all different artists and styles, meaning that there is artwork for everyone to enjoy. If you’d like to purchase artwork, everything is discounted, which makes the purchase even easier.

If you’re at all interested in theatre, visit Triton Gallery to view a beautiful display of window cards dating back almost a hundred years. These collectibles are in very good condition and serve as an amazing way to view the history of Broadway. If you were interested in purchasing one of these window cards, the prices here are reasonable as well.

For such a big neighborhood, it may come as a surprise that there is only one university located in Midtown West. Nonetheless, The Graduate Center resides in the area, and as a City University of New York, offers a great public school education for a lower cost. This CUNY delivers most of the doctoral degrees of all of the universities, and thus is the premier provider of public graduate education. The school awards a variety of degrees, ranging from business to theatre, and includes master’s and doctoral programs. Therefore, the Graduate Center, located in Midtown West, is a marvelous option for a graduate university right in New York City.

Herald Square Park

In Midtown West there are many fabulous parks for you to enjoy. First, you can take a stroll through Herald Square, a beautiful monument that epitomizes the beauty of New York City. This is a great retreat from the department stores like Forever 21, H&M, and Macy’s, so you can sit for a quiet lunch during your break from shopping. This is definitely a destination to explore when you’re in the area.

Another tremendous park is Pier 84 Park, a perfect place to get away from the hustle and bustle of NYC while still in the center of the action. The park has tables and chairs at the end of the pier that are awesome to sit on and watch the boats go by, a grassy patch that you’ll love to lie on to sunbathe during the warmer months, and a dog park. Don’t forget about the great view of the Manhattan skyline, or you may miss out on an opportunity to view New York City like you’ve never done before.

There are many fantastic parks to enjoy while in Midtown West, so you shouldn’t miss out.
