West Harlem has diversified in recent years but has still retained its neighborhood feel. Classic brownstones can be found throughout, and these architectural gems speak to the neighborhood’s proud history.

Originally a Dutch settlement named after the city of Haarlem in The Netherlands, modern Harlem was purchased from the Lenape tribe in 1658. Since then it has gone through many cultural revolutions, all of which have added their flavor to the neighborhood. Since its inception, the Dutch, English, Jewish and Italian residents that have called it home have all left their indelible marks. In the early 20th century, however, the demographics changed when many African Americans migrated to the area during The Great Migration.

Harlem real estate has grown in popularity, especially amongst those searching for great deals in Manhattan real estate. West Harlem apartments and neighborhood may soon be transformed from a diamond in the rough to one of NYC’s trendier neighborhoods, with its real estate values skyrocketing. Harlem has long been a center for the arts, restaurants and exciting nightlife options, so it is perfect for someone interested in the vibrancy of New York City.

Pisticci

Pisticci is an upscale Italian restaurant that has been serving the West Harlem community for years. The decor of the restaurant will make you feel like you are dining in Italy. The food is fresh and delicious, and you should definitely try Michael pasta, fig salad,ravioli with spinach, and coconut cake for dessert.

If you’re craving sushi, we’ve got you covered. Geisha Sushi is a great Japanese restaurant with an awesome sushi bar. The place has so many roll options like Mummy Roll, Crazy Friday Roll, and Golden Apple roll, but there are also many more. Pick out one of these eccentric rolls to try today!

For arguably the best Cuban restaurant not only in Harlem but all of New York City, try out Harlem’s Floridita. The food is so delicious you won’t get tired of this place. The ropa vieja is a must, as well as the excellent lechon asado. The seafood appetizer – tostones con mariscos – is mouthwatering, so try some today!


If you’re planning to throw a BBQ for friends and family, Dinosaur Bar-B-Que is the place to order from. They also provide catering services for parties and big corporate events. Dinosaur Bar-B-Que is nationally acclaimed, as their food and sauces have won honors in publications such as Men’s Health and Eating Well magazines.

If you are desperate for some Chinese takeout, New Double Dragon is a great choice. The service is very quick, and delivery arrives within 20-25 minutes. And, if you want to eat healthy, you can customize your order; for example, you can order your fried rice made with brown rice instead of white or order the foods steamed instead of fried (even the general tso’s tofu).

For a delicious chicken and waffles place in Harlem, check out Amy Ruth’s Restaurant, located right off West 116th Street. They offer amazing critically-acclaimed American food, and with 24-hour service, this may be the most convenient restaurant in West Harlem.

Hamilton Grange National Memorial

With many attractions to choose from, you’ll never be bored in West Harlem.

First, you have to visit Grant’s Tomb for a needed dose of history. Also known as General Grant National Memorial, this site is the final resting place of Ulysses S Grant, the 18th president of the United States. Grant’s Tomb is the second largest mausoleum in the Western Hemisphere, so its surely a sight to witness for yourself.

The Church of the Intercession is located at 550 West 155th Street between Broadway and Amsterdam Ave if you’re interested in some spiritual identity. At Intercession you will be surrounded by a beautiful building, but more importantly in an open and welcoming community.

If you are interested in American History you should definitely visit Hamilton Grange National Memorial, a National Park Service Site in St. Nicholas Park. This memorial has actually preserved the relocated home of U.S. Founding Father Alexander Hamilton, which is very interesting to view a piece of living history.

Regardless of the attractions you choose from, West Harlem will surely have a fun and enjoyable experience for you.

Many artists from Europe and other parts of the world have showcased their work at this gallery, yielding a truly international experience. This is a very rare chance to see the expression of a culture that is very distinct from our own, so do not miss out on this opportunity!

Or, check out the Sol Studio, which has terrific events for the creative and the artistic community. The Sol Studio celebrates the creativity of honored and emerging artists from around the world and engages the public with creative expressions in the form of artwork. This hip location is definitely the place to visit if you’re interested in undiscovered artists.

Hamilton Heights School

There are 14 major public schools located in West Harlem, and the majority of schools meet the Adequate Yearly Progress standards measured by New York State Department of Education. As an up-and-coming area, the educational progression of the neighborhood is improving every year.

One of the premier schools is Hamilton Heights School. Located on 1750 Amsterdam Ave, this elementary school offers a very high quality education. At this school, all second-through-fifth graders are required to take Arabic language classes, making it the first of its kind in New York City to integrate Arabic into the curriculum.

The PS 208 Alaine Locke School is part of a federal magnet grant program that seeks to add diversity to Harlem schools, and the school is focused on moving kids toward college. Although the school requires an application, once your child is accepted, they will receive a great caliber of education.


In terms of universities, West Harlem also contains some of the best of New York City.

The premier university of the neighborhood is Columbia University, which has been here for more than 250 years. Columbia is one of the best universities of the country, and as an Ivy League School, attracts many bright students that diversify the area positively and create an educational environment.

Also located in West Harlem, City College of New York offers outstanding teaching, learning and research. With outstanding programs in architecture, engineering, education, and the liberal arts and sciences, City College of New York prepares students for the future, and produces outstanding leaders in every field.

Columbia University

The Touro College of Osteopathic Medicine is a private, American osteopathic medical school. It was the first medical school to open in New York State in nearly 30 years and is the first osteopathic medical school with a special emphasis on training minority doctors. Attendance at this university is a great opportunity to receive an education in an intimate setting and attention given to each student.

West Harlem Piers

West Harlem is a centerpiece for many parks of the area, which creates beautiful greenery within the confines of New York City.

West Harlem Piers features a landscaped transformation of the historic Manhattanville shoreline. The natural and artificial features of this site form a valley and small sheltered cove off the Hudson River, which is a perfect spot for an intimate gathering among friends or place to relax.

Rising 69 feet above the Hudson River, Riverbank State Park offers a wide variety of recreational, athletic, and arts experiences for all ages, interests, and abilities. They have an Olympic-sized pool, skating rink, theatre, and restaurant all for your enjoyment while at the park.

Jackie Robinson Park is bordered by 145th St. and 155th St., and by Bradhurst Ave and Edgecombe Ave. There is a huge swimming pool on the South End of the park, which is a main attraction, and you can also find live music in the park every weekend.

Therefore, West Harlem offers a diverse array of parks to choose from, which is integral in a city environment.
