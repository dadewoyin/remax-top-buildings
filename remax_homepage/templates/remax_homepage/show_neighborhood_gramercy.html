Gramercy is a safe and quiet approximately rectangular-shaped area named after the famous fenced-in private park Gramercy Park. The neighborhood is located next to Stuyvesant Town, Flatiron, and Midtown East, giving it great proximity to many nightlife options while still maintaining its private feel.

The main attraction for those visiting the area is the famous Gramercy Park. Known for its privacy, only residents living around the park have a key to enter the facility. Therefore, Gramercy Park is renowned for being the only true private park in Manhattan. Even if you don’t live around its perimeter, you will love the view from outside, as Gramercy Park is on the U.S. National Register of Historic Places.

Gramercy Park sets the tone for the entire neighborhood, which takes after its private and serene aura. The quaint shops and restaurants that dot the area are very charming, and Gramercy even offers elegant nightlife options for you to enjoy. Also, while walking the streets of Gramercy you’ll be able to view original townhouses of Greek Revival, Italianate, Gothic Revival and Victorian Gothic styles.

Even with its magical appeal, the median prices for Gramercy are not much more expensive than the average New York City rental, making this an affordable and practical option for many prospective buyers/tenants. With 5 subway lines converging around the neighborhood, it also features convenient transportation, which leaves us with one question: what doesn’t Gramercy have?

Gramercy Park

One of the most serene and beautiful places in all of New York City is hidden in Gramercy, namely the Gramercy Park.

This park gains much of its serenity due to its privacy: there is no public access to the park, and only residents directly surrounding the area are awarded a key. In fact, there are only 383 keys in total, so most people who walk by the park cannot stop in for a peaceful visit.

Besides the rules of privacy, Gramercy Park also doesn’t allow photography, and commercial photos of the park were not released until a guest unintentionally released them against park rules. Besides the exclusivity of the interior, the park also mandates that there are no pets, no smoking, and other regulations.

If you want to be sure that you can gain access to the park, however, all hope is not lost: if you stay at the Gramercy Park Hotel, they will escort you for private access. Otherwise, you’ll only be able to look from the outside wishing you had a key.
