A diverse neighborhood located uptown, Washington Heights is one of the hidden gems of Manhattan. This neighborhood is bordered by Hamilton Heights to the South and Inwood to the North.

One of the perks of living in Washington Heights is its beautiful backdrop. In fact, three parks are located within the small neighborhood, most notably Fort Tryon Park. At Fort Tryon Park, you’ll be able to relax with a picnic, serene run through the park, or even curling up with a book to enjoy the views of the George Washington Bridge. Of course, the parks are not the only attractions here, as you can visit some diverse art galleries and performing art centers that showcase the talents of Washington Heights residents. Whatever you choose, you will definitely enjoy your time in Washington Heights.

In terms of real estate prices, Washington Heights is significantly cheaper than the New York City average for both rentals and sales. Even so, the prices are rising, so be sure to get a piece of the action while the prices are very reasonable if you’d like a great investment for the future. As a very desirable location for young professionals and independent thinkers, you should certainly consider Washington Heights for your next property.

New Leaf Restaurant & Bar

The fine dining of Washington Heights will surely amaze, with opportunities to try everything from Italian to Spanish-American fusion.

For a tasty treat, check out New Leaf Restaurant & Bar, which creates delicious dishes and big portions for lunch. Customer love the Challah French toast especially, but everything on the menu will strike your fancy. Come see what you’re missing at New Leaf Restaurant & Bar!

Or, to try what many consider the best restaurant uptown, try Beans and Vines Fine Dining. Start off your meal with a sample of their great selection of wine and beer, which will complement the amazing food offered here. Some dishes of choice include Yuca fries and Gourmet Chimi, but feel free to try anything on the menu! Make sure you try Beans and Vines while in Washington Heights.

If you want to try some of the best pizza in Washington Heights, Antika Restaurant & Pizzeria is your destination. Upon ordering, you will be served a huge pie, which you should pair with a wine of choice. If you’re not in the mood for pizza, the pasta and other Italian food here is also excellent.


If you want to try an American-Caribbean fusion, check out Pick and Eat. The food here is very healthy and fresh, with many vegetarian items on the menu. You can substitute virtually any ingredient here for one more to your taste, ensuring you find something perfect to eat!

Or, visit La Barca Restaurant if you’ve never sampled Dominican food before and are interested. The 24/7 service is very convenient for a late-night snack, and the cheap food provides you the option to order from here often. With quick delivery, you can receive the healthy dish of your choice in no time, so order from La Barca today.

For some authentic Thai food, Tung Thong Thai Restaurant is definitely for you. The quick and excellent service is perfect for lunch, which features an affordable lunch special with a ton of food. Tung Thong also offers many vegetarian options, so the restaurant will please even those with dietary restrictions.

Morris Jumel Museum

If you’d like to view a little bit of history shrouded in mystery, Morris-Jumel Mansion is the best place to visit. Located in Washington Heights, this home is the oldest surviving house in NYC, making it well-known as the haunted house of the neighborhood. Whether you’re interested in becoming a ghostbuster or just enjoying the beauty of historical architecture, Morris-Jumel Mansion will surely amaze you.

Or, for some pleasant music, visit Jazz at Marjorie Eliot’s, located in Marjorie Eliot’s historic apartment. The musicians here are very talented, and the neighborhood environment really connects friends and strangers alike within this small space. If you’re in need of some warmth and comfort, Marjorie Eliot’s is your spot.

Perhaps Sister’s Uptown Bookstore would be a great place for you to try. Whether you’d like to buy a book from the community bookstore or sip coffee at the cafe, you will love the performances and culture created at this establishment. There are also various pieces of art for you to enjoy while visiting this location, so check it out today!


The art of Washington Heights is primarily historic, so check out some amazing cultural movements from past times.

First, visit Hispanic Society of America to view artwork from 16th century Spanish and Latin American collections. This escape to another time is definitely the hidden gem of Washington Heights, and you will marvel at the artwork and differences between the various Hispanic art movements.

Or, for another portal to another time, check out The Cloisters. Featuring medieval art & architecture, this art gallery provides you will all the materials, including sculptures, paintings, and books, to make you feel a part of this time period. The Cloisters also offers tours of the gallery for a more informed perspective on what you’re looking at.

Fort Tryon Park

Washington Heights offers some superb parks due to its suburb-like environment.

First, visit Fort Tryon Park for a great view of the Hudson River, and George Washington Bridge. The gardens here are beautiful, and also features a winding path for bikers and runners. If you’d like to picnic, Fort Tryon is your location, as gorgeous grassy areas here are waiting for a delightful picnic.

Or, check out Jay Hood Wright Park if you have children that would like to play on a playground, or even a dog that could utilize the dog park. With a little bit of everything, including sports grounds, barbecue areas, and grassy areas to host special events at, Jay Hood Wright Park is the perfect mixture of everything a good park should have. Come check out this location today! Or, Highbridge Park features a swimming pool, concerts, and beauty in its stunning location on the East River. Residents love the mountain bike course, as well as skate park that is world class. Visit this park if you’re looking for something diverse and entertaining to fill up your day.
