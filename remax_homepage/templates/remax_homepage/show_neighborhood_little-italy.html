Little Italy is one of the most classic neighborhoods in New York City, bordered by SoHo, Chinatown, and Nolita.

This active neighborhood earned its name because of the high concentration of Italian immigrants that used to occupy the area. Today, Little Italy’s commerce centers around its restaurants and tourism, which are staples to this area because of its fantastic legacy and cultural history. Here you can find some of the best Italian restaurants in all of New York City, so if you’re looking for some genuine Little Italy pizza, you’ve come to the right place. Unsurprisingly, Italian food is basically the only cuisine that you can find here, but due to its close proximity to Chinatown, an entirely different culture is just a few blocks away.

The heart of Little Italy lies in the residents, and while the neighborhood may be named after Italians, the area is becoming more and more diversified. While there is a significant population of Little Italy lifers, the turnaround rates for properties are high, to make way for an incoming younger generation. Still, property values are above the New York City average, meaning that the area is definitely still popular and in demand. If you want to be part of a cultural turning point in Little Italy, now is your chance to rent/buy!

So, whether you’re interested in Little Italy pizzas or the iconic atmosphere of the neighborhood, Little Italy is the perfect place for you to call home.

The fine dining scene in Little Italy includes Italian, Italian, and more Italian food. While there are many tourist locations, you can easily find an authentic restaurant with some amazing food.

First, dine at Pellegrino’s Italian Restaurant if you’re looking to have a great night of food and fun. Try one of their fresh pasta dishes from their menu, or opt to request any Italian dish and they will make it for you. This impeccable service and location really make for a lovely night, complete with their amazing desserts.

Or, for the freshest pasta of the area, try Angelo’s, an institution of Little Italy. They prepare fresh pasta every day, and furnished with their quality sauces, the dishes here are amazing.

Be sure to stop by Ferrara Bakery and Cafe if you haven’t already had dessert. Known especially for their cannolis, Ferrara also offers a variety of pastries made fresh and delicious. Try the chocolate truffle cake if you like chocolate, or indulge in some of their New York Cheesecake.


If you’re dining in Little Italy, the obvious favorite cuisine is Italian. Here, you’ll find the best in the city if you’re looking for a good meal.

For a classic Italian meal that brings old school flavor to all of its dishes, choose Benito One. In addition to the homey, authentic feel of the restaurant, the pastas that Benito produces are amazing and sure to please. Be sure to try some scrumptious bruschetta to start off your meal.

Or, for a high energy Italian joint that is sure to leave you excited about your meal, visit La Mela. They offer generous portions of their amazing pasta dishes and appetizers, with plenty of drinks to boot.

While Little Italy is a small area to find many grocery stores, those that exist within the neighborhood are high-quality and satisfying.

Try New York Mart if you’re looking for a quick and efficient store to shop at without the hassle of big crowds. Furnished with an Asian influence from the surrounding neighborhood, New York Mart offers all basic items for everyday needs, as well as specialty foods to prepare Asian dishes.

Or, for an amazing Italian deli with the classic look and feel, visit Di Palo’s. They offer fresh cuts of many Italian meats, as well as high-quality cheese with a wide variety. They also have other items for sale, such as pre-made pasta, coffee, and Italian spices, making this a great stop for making an Italian meal.

Therefore, shopping in Little Italy is easy with the conveniences of terrific grocery stores, all set conveniently within the small neighborhood.

Capita

Besides the great food, there are many things to do in Little Italy while you’re enjoying the neighborhood.

First, as a tribute to the neighborhood, check out the Italian American Museum. They display many different Italian artifacts, such as old Italian newspapers in America, and various pieces of artwork constructed by Italian Americans. The goal of this museum is to celebrate the culture of Italian Americans who immigrated years ago, and the impact that they have on the world and Little Italy neighborhood.

If you’re in Little Italy at the right time, perhaps you will catch the San Gennaro Festival. Held annually, this festival offers a ton of food and entertainment for you to enjoy all day. The festival goes on for 11 days of parades, games, and lots of fun. Be sure to check out this homage to the authentic Italian era today.

Or, catch an event at Capitale, a great event space in Little Italy with a beautiful ballroom and catering services. They can make an amazing event for any occasion, and add a sense of elegance and charm to the evening.

So, whatever your style or needs may be, Little Italy has the perfect store waiting for you.
