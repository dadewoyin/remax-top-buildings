Although perhaps the smallest neighborhood in all of New York City, Central Park South is definitely one of the most important. Its span of two blocks offers some of the most desirable real estate-as well as the most expensive-due to the distinguished Central Park views and amazing location.

At the edge of Central Park South lies Columbus Circle, which contains some fabulous shopping centers and novelties to utilize. If you’re more in the mood for walking than shopping, the South End of Central Park provides beautiful grounds to wander around. There are also other modes of transportation in Central Park, such as carriage rides and pedicabs, which could be a more fun way to tour Central Park.

Central Park South’s Midtown location gives it ideal proximity to both the business-centered downtown and the residential uptown, which is perfect regardless of where you’re travelling. The prices are certainly above the New York City average for both rentals and sales, and only continue to increase. As one of the most desirable neighborhoods of New York City, you’ll certainly have to fight to get a good price on one of these properties, but it is definitely worth the hassle.

So whether you’re enjoying the view one the rooftop of a Central Park South building, shopping in Columbus Circle, or walking around in the park itself, Central Park South will leave you with a sense of opulence and pleasure.

Marea

With its prime location right on Central Park, the restaurants of Central Park South are often very luxurious and expensive. You will certainly find some fabulous dining in this neighborhood!

First, if you’re looking for a fantastic seafood restaurant with high accolades, check out Marea. They offer a wide variety of seafood and meat dishes, and have Italian influences of pasta and antipasti to accompany them. The most talked about dish here is the octopus fusilli with bone marrow, but every item on the menu is a delight.

Or, for some tremendous Italian with beautiful views of Central Park, visit Villagio on the Park. With several different dish options, including an abundance of meat and seafood, you will surely leave here stuffed and satisfied. Villagio is easily one of the best Italian restaurants of New York City.

Regardless of what you choose, the fine dining of Central Park South will definitely impress.


Although it is hard to find fast and easy food within the bustle of Central Park South, there are a few superb options if you are short on time.

For some teriffic pizza, check out Angelo’s Coal Oven Pizzeria. They make an exemplary crust and marinara sauce, producing an amazing balance of flavors. Angelo’s also offers a full bar to enjoy with your meal.

Or, if instead you want a quick bite to eat, try Angela’s Sandwich Shop. This small store can be easily missed, but the sandwiches are high quality and affordable. Also, Angela’s is a great option for breakfast, as they make quick and cheap selections.

FIKA

If you’re looking for a pastry and some coffee or hot chocolate to take with you on a stroll through Central Park, FIKA is your place. This Swedish coffeehouse creates fresh coffee, chocolates, and other treats, all within walking distance of Central Park.

So, there are many things to do in Central Park South, both within Central Park itself and the surrounding areas.

South End of Central Park

The reason why Central Park South is so expensive is because of the views of Central Park. Because of the wonderful location, everyone in the neighborhood is within a five minute walk of the greatest park of New York City. With so much to see and do, the possibilities are endless for a fantastic day in the park.

On the South End of Central Park, there are many fun activities that you can enjoy. If you love architecture, check out the many statues that are sprinkled throughout the park, including Simon Bolivar, Jose Julian Marti, and Christopher Columbus. There are also beautiful places to enjoy a stroll through the park, such as the Olmstead Flower Bed and Hallett Nature Sanctuary.

Or, if you are more active, then you can stop by the Wollman Rink for ice skating in the winter, while during the summer you can lay out in Sheep Meadow to feel the sun on your face. Complete with playgrounds, sports equipment and areas, plenty of spots to eat, as well as venues to gather at for events, the South End of Central Park has everything you could possible wish for.
