Pig & Khao

The Lower East Side may be best known to you as the home of rowdy nightlife, but that adventure extends to the food scene as well.

You can spot Top Chef Contestant Leah Cohen at her restaurant Pig & Khao, which mixes Thai and Filipino influences. Pork lovers will enjoy this restaurant, as their signature dishes are pork head, belly and leg. Their small bowls of beer served piggy style on the side will make your meal more than perfect!

Like some other punk-rock places in this neighborhood, Contra has very little signage out front to help you find it. But you’ll forgive them once you do encounter the restaurant, because their tasting menu is the rare arrangement that won’t make you forfeit rent: it costs a mere $55 for 5 courses. Their amazing variety of options and tastes will leave you coming back for more.

You don’t have to fight about where to eat for a group of people with various ideas: The Fat Radish is ideal for the vegetarians, but placates the carnivores with a killer bacon cheeseburger and pork chop. Your group will also agree on the gorgeously bright, all-white space, which perfectly complements the delicious food.

If you’re looking for some quick and easy food in the Lower East Side, the area will definitely not disappoint. Takeout is a lovely option for this area due to the very small and crowded restaurant locations.

Looking for an exceptional slice of pizza at reasonable prices in the Lower East Side? La Margarita is the best place to grab a quality slice. The pizza is thin, crispy, and surely tasty. The best part of La Margarita is that if you get there before 6 P.M., you can snag a slice for only $2.00! Try out some of this cheap and easy pizza today.

Mezetto

If you are looking for something a little more extravagant, try Mezetto. They serve a unique combination of American staples infused with Mediterranean flavors. Known for their lamb meatballs, truffled gnocchi, and chicken shawarma shishlik, they will take you on a tour of flavors from all over the Mediterranean– Italian, Israeli, Greek and beyond.

For some handcrafted puddings and hot or cold bubble tea, choose Pomme LES. The puddings of choice are mango, green tea, and chocolate banana pudding, which are all sure to steal your heart. They also serve sweet and savory crepes, which are absolutely delicious. Bring a friend to enjoy some tempting foods there!

Copper & Oak

If you’d like to enjoy some great nightlife, you’ve come to the right place-the Lower East Side has some well established bars that you will surely enjoy.

The best bar for live music would be Mercury Lounge. They don’t just provide well-known acts, but also new performances. The famous band named Strokes got their start at this intimate, divey venue. Moreover, it’s always easy to get a beer as there’s a separate mini-bar in the music room.

If you are a big fan of whiskey, choose Copper & Oak, a fancy whiskey house from Brandy Library’s Flavien Desoblin with more than 600 bottles crammed on backlit bookshelves. Vintage whiskey is always there for you to explore, or you could expand your wisdom with barkeepers’ guidance.

Loreley is a wise choice for a gathering among friends. This mellow lil’ beer garden has a tucked-away outdoor seating area, gigantic brews, as well as normal-sized, but highly enjoyable, German fare.

Whole Foods Market

For a great grocery location, the Lower East Side will definitely please you with its wide array of stores.

First, Essex Street Market mainly offers a wide variety of culinary products including gourmet cheese, premium cuts of meat, fresh fish, and general grocery items. Some people love this place because of their distinctive foreign country foods, which they can buy here without a hassle. The merchants there are very friendly and create a welcoming, community-oriented environment.

And, Whole Foods Market is no doubt your best choice to shop for very fresh foods and high quality grocery items. In fact, the Lower East Side location is commended to be the best Whole Foods in Manhattan. The large space and beautiful layout will bring you a joyful shopping experience, as well as the extra amenities of second floor seating and free Wi-Fi for two hours.

Another great stop is Union Market, known for their organic and natural foods, and this store was the first Gold Certificated LEED Green grocery in New York. You could even find a wonderful grab-and-go breakfast deal there in the morning.
