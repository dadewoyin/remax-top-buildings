Known for its diverse property values and housing situations, Turtle Bay is a neighborhood in Midtown Manhattan that extends from 41st to 53rd Street and from Lexington Avenue to the East River. Turtle Bay is bordered by Murray Hill, Midtown, and Lenox Hill.

One of the perks of living in Turtle Bay is the proximity to Midtown, yet quieter backdrop for everyday living. Even though the neighborhood is relatively small, Turtle Bay has many different dining options, from small cafes to Michelin-star restaurants. Come nighttime, the area isn’t dead, as the younger generation that resides here parties in the plentiful bars and clubs, most notably during Turtle Bay NYC happy hour.

During the day, you can find all the essentials in Turtle Bay-great shopping locations, excellent laundry services, and local food markets. Because of all the fantastic amenities, Turtle Bay is a very livable neighborhood for people of all different walks of life, as there are laundry services on-the-go for the busy professional, as well as local grocery selections for a relaxed retiree. Regardless of your age or living style, Turtle Bay may be the perfect choice for you.

The variety of this neighborhood attracts many different styles, ranging from those who settle in small rental apartments to the luxurious penthouses of the area’s center. Therefore, many different price points are available to buy or rent in Turtle Bay, making this a reasonable option for virtually anyone interested. Be sure to consider Turtle Bay in your next property hunt, as you may be surprised at the caliber of homes in your price range!

Sushi Yasuda

For dining in Turtle Bay, you will be presented with many options to suit your needs.

A very upscale restaurant in Turtle Bay is Four Seasons Restaurant, which features mid-century architecture on the exterior, and beautifully designed rooms inside. The menus change by season, so you can try something new in the winter, spring, summer, and fall. They also feature many rooms, such as the Pool Room, which is more open and romantic, as well as the Grill Room, known for its power lunches.

If you’re trying to find the best sushi of New York City, Sushi Yasuda might be the winner. Their simplified approach of focusing on the freshness of the rice and fish emphasizes the simplicity of excellent cuisine. They also have a homemade maki roll, created with individualized sushi rolls, that are shipped from Japan exclusively for Sushi Yasuda.

For arguably the best steakhouse of Midtown East, visit Wolfgang’s Steakhouse. Although the cuts here are very expensive, they are well worth the price, as customers rave about their quality. If you’re in a group, then order the porterhouse steak to share, which will be more than enough meat for your party. And, be sure to add side dishes, which embellish the steak to new heights.


For anyone looking for takeout, Turtle Bay hosts plenty of institutions to choose from.

If you’re in the mood for mouthwatering Greek food, check out Uncle Gussys. Even though it appears to be a simple food cart, don’t let its appearance deceive you; this is one of the most popular lunch stops in the area. You’ll have to get in a long line to try one of their many Greek options, from Souvlaki skewers to Greek salads and even a great Philly cheesesteak if you wish to try something different.

For an exotic Cambodian dish to change up your normal routine, try Num Pang Sandwich Shop. Their gourmet food creates many bold flavors, and their meats are always very tender, Dishes of choice here include their 5 spice pork belly sandwich and coconut tiger shrimp, but don’t let that deter you from trying another menu item.

A Provence-style artisanal restaurant located in Turtle Bay is Chicken Provence, which features health-conscious gourmet food that is both delicious and affordable. Receiving GQ’s Dish of the year award, Chicken Provence combines the delicacies of French cuisine with tender, juicy meats.

The Center for Fiction

For some lively entertainment and museums, try visiting any of the venues of Turtle Bay.

First, The Center for Fiction supplies the area with a lovely literary collection. Created for the betterment of the Turtle Bay neighborhood, this library focuses on fictional plotlines that please children and adults alike. The library here is very diverse, and all of the carefully selected books are great reads. With a large space, you can enjoy a book right in the library itself! The Center for Fiction is also rentable for special occasions if you’re really in love with the space.

Or, another library in Turtle Bay is Morgan Library, which puts a more historical spin on reading. Within their collections, they offer old copies of Gutenberg Bibles and historical manuscripts, as well as many other artifacts from history. Other amenities within Morgan Library include a formal dining room and gorgeous architecture throughout. The Morgan Library has been designated a National Historic Landmark, so come admire its beauty today!


The art galleries of Turtle Bay are also top-notch, supplying some amazing works within the small neighborhood.

Visit the iconic Museum of Modern Art if you’re interested in seeing some of the best works of modern times. Some possible artists here include Picasso, Van Gogh, Matisse, and Seurat, with an entire room dedicated to Van Gogh’s Starry Night Painting. The Museum of Modern Art is very popular, so you’ll be met with crowds and lines if you’re looking to see the most popular paintings. Come check out these amazing exhibits that will surely take your breath away.
