From predominantly German to Irish to Hispanic, Manhattan Valley is a very dynamic neighborhood located on the Upper West Side. Manhattan Valley is bordered by Upper West Side and Morningside Heights.

Recently, Manhattan Valley has gained popularity because of its rising social scene and growing economy. Characterized by the availability of green spaces, great dining spots, and bars, Manhattan Valley offers both a peaceful location and a party in one. Older residents can enjoy the diverse fine dining options, while younger students love to party at the many bars and clubs in the area. Even though the area is not known for its attractions, there are many fun things to do-from a museum tour to visiting an art gallery to shopping at one of the many stores-that attract residents and tourists alike.

The easy access of Manhattan Valley really increases its desirability, as it intersects three subway lines within the small area. There are a rising number of Columbia and Barnard students from Morningside Heights that are moving to Manhattan Valley, adding to the appeal of a younger generation in the neighborhood. Therefore, the real estate market for Manhattan Valley has begun to boom, creating a high demand for these cheaper Manhattan Valley apartments. Flor de Mayo

If you’re looking for some fine dining in the Manhattan Valley, the choices are diverse.

For a fantastic American restaurant with amazing soups and salads, try eating at Buster’s in Manhattan Valley. Their service is almost as high-quality as the food, as the servers strive to create a comforting vibe while serving you amazing food. With use of only local food and fresh ingredients, this restaurant should top your list for food to try in Manhattan Valley.

An exceptional stop for Chino-Latino fare is Flor de Mayo, where you can find a combination of Chinese food and Mexican food that will definitely be delicious. From sweet plantains to yellow rice to roasted chicken, whatever style you try will amaze you at the blend of flavors. To top it off, the service here is amazing, ensuring that you will have a lovely time here.

You’ve probably never tried Malaysian food before, so now is a great time to sample some at Malaysia Grill. They are most famous here for their coconut rice and roti canai, a Malaysian flatbread influenced by Indian flavors. This hidden treasure will surely provide you with an unforgettable new cuisine experience, so try some of their signature dishes today!


If you’re up for trying some cultural food, Manhattan Valley is your place to eat! Their streets are packed with restaurants of all variety, ensuring that you can get a quality meal of all cuisines.

First, if you want a marvelous place to eat out from, try Saiguette. This Vietnamese restaurant serves some of the best banh mi in New York City, and its moon cakes and pho are also amazing! The freshness of the food is unparalleled, so you will surely enjoy an affordable Vietnamese treat at this restaurant.

Or, if you’re in the mood for an authentic spicy meal, eat at Szechuan Garden. Their food is among the best Chinese of the Upper West Side due to its amazing quality and service. You will not be disappointed with this meal, as long as you can take the heat!

Saiguette

For a quick bite to eat where you’re sure to get a great bang for your buck, visit the Indian restaurant Doaba Deli. For $8, you get a choice of four curries and sides, ensuring that you will be full and satisfied for the price. They offer terrific vegetarian options as well, complete with tea and desserts to try.

Lion's Head

The bars of Manhattan Valley offer tastes for many styles, so try some amazing drinks and bar food today!

First, check out Amsterdam Tavern if you’re hungry and in the mood for a drink. With amazing nachos and sweet potato fries, as well as an extensive beer list, you will surely find exactly the taste you are looking for. Amsterdam Tavern is not very crowded nor expensive, so you can even enjoy this bar on a budget.

Or, if you’re more in the mood for a dive bar, then Lion’s Head may be perfect for you. The food here of choice are the tater tots, which you can savor along with some cheap beer and a great selection. Complete with a relaxed setting and friendly staff, Lion’s Head has all the tools you need for a fun night.

Another interesting find is The dam, which offer specials throughout the week on different drinks, such as Martini Mondays and Tequila Thursdays. Be sure to check out the website ahead of time to find which day your favorite drink is discounted! If you want some food with your drinks, parmesan truffle fries are always a tremendous option, or ask the friendly bartender for his suggestion.

Westside Market

One of the best markets in the Manhattan Valley is Westside Market, a 24 hour food market that sells almost everything you need. They have a large variety of fresh fruit and vegetables, cooked food, a huge selection of cheese, a salad bar, and hot meals. The friendly employees and efficient clerks make your shopping experience here very enjoyable.

Another upscale market is Garden of Eden. This stop is perfect for Columbia students, as they offer a 10% student discount and a half price food bar after 8pm. Some other perks of this grocery store are its proximity to many transit lines, as well as great prices and organic food selection.

Of course, a nationally recognized brand is Whole Foods Market, where you can be guaranteed to get incredible quality of products and a wide selection of foods. With a wine store, salad bar, and distinguished prepared food section, this Whole Foods has all the amenities that you would expect and want from a grocery store.

Nicholas Roerich Museum

The many attractions of the Manhattan Valley area keep residents and tourists alike coming back for more.

First, you should definitely visit Nicholas Roerich Museum, a museum that holds Roerich’s artwork, which combines Russian and Asian influences to create a beautiful and unique art selection. With free admission and kind staff that truly care about the art, you will certainly find Nicholas Roerich Museum inspirational and beautiful, so check it out today!

Or, if you’re looking for a luxurious way to tour New York City, Vivatours USA can certainly facilitate a tour tailored exactly to your tastes. With guides that speak English, Spanish, and Portuguese, you will be able to find a driver and tour guide to converse with and take you to various locations, including shopping, attractions, dinners, and sporting events. What better way to enjoy your visit to Manhattan Valley?

For a display of talent and performance, check out The West End, a lounge that offers many different performances for your viewing pleasure. The production and performers here are very professional, so you will definitely love one of their shows along with some tasty snacks and beer. Residents love enjoying a night here of relaxation and fun, and you will as well.


Manhattan Valley offers some beautiful artwork for your viewing pleasure.

Alan Klotz Gallery is an extraordinary option if you’re interested in viewing 19th and 20th century vintage photography. As a member of the Association of International Photography Art Dealers, this gallery is a professional buyer and seller of artwork in the New York City area. The gallery also offers collector’s seminars and auctions for even greater interaction with the artwork.

Or, Gail Martin Gallery provides many fantastic viewing opportunities, with a display of ancient, antique, and ethnographic textiles. Many of these textiles come from exotic locations, like Central Asia, Africa, Pre-Columbian Peru, and other historical regions, combined with the work of contemporary artists.
