Top 10 - Downtown




1. 56 Leonard

56 Leonard Street in Tribeca stands 60 stories on top of an Anish Kapoor sculpture similar to his Cloud Gate sculpture from 2006, in Chicago. The 145 unit residence was designed by Pritzker Prize-winning architects Herzog & de Meuron, and developed by Alexico Group & Hines. Alexico Group & Hines are credited for the unique shape of the building, as it is reminiscent of a Jenga puzzle.

Along with the sculpture at the front side of the building, 56 Leonard has many amenities. The building has a library lounge, and indoor theatre, and a 75-foot infinity edge pool. Building services include yoga studio, private dining salon, and a treatment and steam room.




2. Walker Tower

Walker Tower on 212 West 18th Street in Chelsea made famously designed by Ralph Walker, follows his model of having leveled façades facing the front of the tower. The building was completed in 1929, but since has been transformed into a 47-luxury condominium residence. Developed by JDS Development Group, a majority of the residences have private terraces including 11 units with their own wood burning fireplaces.

Walker Tower is more than just façades and terraces. Walker Tower includes a fully equipped fitness center, a yoga room, and even a library lounge with bar. Children are also taken care of with a playroom of their own.




3. One Madison

One Madison on 23 East 22nd Street comes third in our ranking. This gorgeous building in The Flatiron District provides views from river-to-river standing 60 stories tall. This building directly oversees Madison Square Park. Developed by The Related Companies, and designed by Cetra Ruddy, the building was completed in 2009.

One Madison has its own club within the residences, known as the One Club. Aside from One Club, amenities include a common recreation parlor, screening rooms as well as a 50-foot lap pool, a fully equipped fitness center, and a spa and steam room.




4. 551 West 21st Street

Developed by Scott Resnick, this lovely 44-unit residence in West Chelsea sits directly across from Chelsea Piers, overlooking the Hudson River. The building will be 19 stories tall where all windows being golden-framed when completed and the top three floors set to be penthouses with terraces as designed by Foster + Partners.

Among the open terraces, the uppermost penthouse includes a 61-foot skypool on its terrace. The other penthouses have their own loggias facing the Hudson River. Building amenities include a state-of-the-art fitness center, spa and steam room, a children’s playroom and a fireplace lounge for residents.




5. 18 Gramercy Park

Completed in 1927 by Zeckerdorf Development, 18 Gramercy Park was recently transformed into a 16-unit condominium within its 19 stories including a rare three level Maisonette with its own private entry. Designed by Robert A.M. Stern Architects, each room is meant to provide sumptuous light and expansive views.

Aside from being on a short list of people with keys to the private Gramercy Park, the building provides many amenities and services. For instance, there are spa rooms with steam and sauna, as well as a fitness center and pet grooming salon. Uniquely, 18 Gramercy Park has a rooftop library and lounge.




6. 40 Bond Street

40 Bond on Bond Street in NoHo, was completed by Ian Schrager in 1950. The exterior design was the work of designer Herzog & de Meuron with the help of EXYD, a German design company, who created the metal gate enclosing the front of 40 Bond with graffiti-like structures. The building is covered in a stainless steel metal that was colored green and supplied by INOX-Color, another German company.

The 33-unit residence has its own in-house housekeeping and childcare services, as well as pet walking and sitting service. The building has a modern fitness center and a spa center. This building is also one of the few who have begun implementing business centers and meeting rooms within the residences.




7. 15 Union Square West

15 Union Square West was transformed by designer Eran Chen of ODA Architecture, and completed in 2008. Chen restored the cast-iron elements from its original development while adding black zinc and glass. Since the transformation, the building now contains 36 residential units, as Chen also added “sky villas” to the top of the building.

Aside from the building’s great location on East 15th Street and Broadway (or Union Square West) right in the heart of Union Square and facing the park, the building provides a spa room, a luxurious common lounge, a fitness center, yoga studio, and 50-foot lap pool.




8. 15 Union Square West

Superior Ink on 400 West 12th Street in West Village was completed in 2009 with a total of 50 apartments as a result of combinations which cut down from the original 68. Developed by The Related Companies and designed by Robert A.M. Architects, the residence reaches 17 stories and, in addition, includes 7 townhouses on Bethune Street. The building is named after the Superior Ink Factory, which was situated on the same address until 2006

Each residence has 10-12 foot ceilings with morado wood 3-inch herringbone flooring. The amenities include a children’s playroom, pilates and yoga room, a full-service fitness center, an entertainment lounge and screening room. The townhouses each have a brick and masonry façade and feature gardens.




9. 10 Madison Square West

This exquisite building on 10 Madison Square West was developed in 1915 by the Vector Group, was designed by Goldstein, Hill & West Architects. This building in The Flatiron District used to be known as the Toy Building until its renovation and renaming in 2013 now holds 125 residential units. The Toy Building was connected by a sky bridge to 200 Fifth Avenue, but hasn’t been used since becoming residential

10 Madison Square West has many great services including a steam and sauna room, fullservice fitness center, spa treatment rooms and a children’s playroom. All while sitting steps away from Madison Square Park and having views on both sides of either the Flatiron Building or the Empire State Building.




10. 40 Mercer Street

Completed in 2006, 40 Mercer Street in SoHo holds a total of 41 units across its uniquely shaped 13 stories. Designed by the Pritzkerprize winning Jean Nouvel, and developed by Hines, Andre Balazs, Jerome Karr, the first 6 floors serve as a pedestal for the 7th floor, which contains a garden and rooftop lounge, and the much narrower upper floors. The exterior incorporates a lot of glass cladding and is colored a dark blue.

40 Mercer sits on an underground parking lot and the T-shaped 50-foot pool, along with the garden and lounge, serve as the key amenities. Along with that, there is a pilates and yoga room, a children’s playroom, a fitness center, and steam and sauna rooms.
